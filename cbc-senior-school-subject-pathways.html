CBC Senior School: Subject Pathways and Career Guidance

👤 KenyaVault Team 📅 June 28, 2026 ⏱️ 4 min read

The Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) Senior School is a critical phase in a student's educational journey. It marks the transition from broad-based learning to specialized pathways that prepare students for higher education and careers. Understanding these pathways is essential for parents, teachers, and students alike.

📌 Key Takeaway: CBC Senior School (Grades 10-12) offers three distinct pathways: Arts & Sports, Social Sciences, and STEM. Each pathway is designed to develop specific competencies and prepare students for their chosen careers.

Understanding CBC Senior School Structure

CBC Senior School spans three years (Grades 10, 11, and 12). Students begin their specialization in Grade 10, building on foundational knowledge from Junior School. The curriculum emphasizes practical skills, critical thinking, and career readiness.

🏛️ KICD Framework: All pathways are aligned with the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD) designs and the national Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) framework.

The Three Pathways

1. Arts and Sports Pathway

This pathway is designed for students with talents and interests in creative arts, performing arts, and sports. It focuses on developing practical skills in various artistic and athletic disciplines.

  • Core Subjects: English, Kiswahili, Mathematics, and Community Service
  • Specialization Options: Visual Arts, Music, Drama, Dance, Sports (Football, Athletics, Swimming, etc.)
  • Career Pathways: Professional athlete, artist, musician, actor, sports coach, arts administrator, entertainment industry professional
  • Higher Education: Universities and colleges offering arts, sports science, and creative arts programs

2. Social Sciences Pathway

This pathway develops students' understanding of human society, cultures, and social institutions. It emphasizes critical analysis, research skills, and an understanding of social dynamics.

  • Core Subjects: English, Kiswahili, Mathematics, and Community Service
  • Specialization Options: History, Geography, Religious Education, Business Studies, Psychology, Sociology
  • Career Pathways: Lawyer, teacher, social worker, journalist, human resources professional, government official, business administrator
  • Higher Education: Law school, teachers' training colleges, universities offering social sciences, business programs

3. STEM Pathway

The Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) pathway prepares students for careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics fields. It emphasizes analytical thinking, problem-solving, and innovation.

  • Core Subjects: English, Kiswahili, Mathematics, and Community Service
  • Specialization Options: Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science, Environmental Science, Agriculture
  • Career Pathways: Doctor, engineer, scientist, IT professional, architect, pharmacist, data analyst, researcher
  • Higher Education: Medical school, engineering schools, universities offering STEM programs, technical institutes

How to Choose the Right Pathway

Choosing a pathway is one of the most important decisions a student will make. Here are some factors to consider:

  • Interests and Passion: What subjects or activities does the student enjoy most? Passion often leads to success.
  • Strengths and Abilities: Assess academic strengths and natural talents in different areas.
  • Career Aspirations: What career does the student envision for their future?
  • Market Demand: Consider job market trends and future opportunities in different fields.
  • Teacher and Counselor Guidance: Seek advice from teachers and career counselors.
⚠️ Important: Students can change pathways within the first year of Senior School if they find their initial choice doesn't align with their interests or strengths. However, it's best to make an informed decision from the start.
